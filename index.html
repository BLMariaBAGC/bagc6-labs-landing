The problem

Training tells people what to do. It never lets them practise doing it.

Most communication training happens in a room, in theory. Then the real moment arrives, with its nerves and its pushback, and the learning does not survive contact with it. People avoid the conversation, or they handle it badly. Either way the cost lands on the team, on the person who left, and on the manager's standing.

What it is

A private rehearsal room for difficult conversations.

01

Speak it or type it

Your person practises with an AI counterpart who responds like a real colleague: defensive, evasive, or upset, and never a script. They can say it out loud, or type it, whichever the moment allows.

02

Get an honest debrief

After each rehearsal they receive a scored account of how they handled it, and what to do differently next time.

03

Practise until it holds

It is on demand and private, so people can get it wrong safely and improve before the conversation counts for real.

The range

The conversations your people cannot afford to get wrong.

  • › Giving hard feedback without breaking the relationship
  • › Holding a position and pushing back on a senior colleague
  • › Handling conflict before it hardens into something worse
  • › Influencing a decision without the authority to command it
  • › Navigating pay, promotion, and career conversations
  • › Listening well when the other person is hard to hear

The outcome

What people take away.

They walk in already rehearsed

The conversation people used to run over in their head at 2am has already happened once, and been adjusted, before it counts for real.

The nerves have somewhere to go

The first, clumsy attempt lands in private. The real conversation is steadier and better judged, because it is no longer the first attempt.

It shows up in the room

Practice builds the muscle, so what someone worked out about handling the moment is actually there when the moment arrives.
